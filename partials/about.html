About this programme

  • Cognitive interventions also known as cognitive retraining or cognitive rehabilitation are known to improve cognitive abilities
  • Is based on principles of neuronal plasticity
  • Different for age groups as well as for different types of brain injury/insult
  • Children with developmental delays respond well to inputs
  • Response allows their developmental trajectories to change

Mechanism of intervention

  • Multiple domains
  • Varying levels of difficulty
  • Several activities are specifically created by the authors for specific clients/diagnostic conditions
  • Activities tap several domains of cognition
  • No one activity can be used exculsively for intervention- always a combination of activities used
  • Activities tap several domains of cognition
  • Has to be tailor made to suit the needs of the client

Domain covered

  • Attention
  • Working memory
  • Mental flexibility
  • Interference control/inhibition control
  • Fluency
  • Visuo-spatial functions
  • Planning
  • Problem solving abilities

Layout for each session

  • A set of five to seven activities are planned for each day
  • Each session typically lasts from 45 minutes to one hour in the beginning and can go up to about 60 to 70 minutes in later stages
  • Targets for each activity in each session is set based on the child’s performance in the previous sessions
  • A minimum of 4 activities must be completed by a given client
  • Each activity is usually carried out for a minimum of 10 minutes.
